My story · 01

From fixing neighborhood PCs
to shipping real products

I've always been the kid who tinkers until things work. That instinct became a career, a company, and a life.

My dad bought me a PC when I was 7 years old. I started with video games. Soon I was the kid fixing computers for friends and neighbors in my town. I realized early that I had a knack for two things: hardware, and working with people.

In college, I competed in Fortnite professionally. I qualified for the FNCS World Series and placed 12th globally. That was my first real paycheck. I built a Twitch audience of 1,000 followers in a single day and became a Twitch Partner.

Gaming gave me two things: the capital to start a company, and the confidence to bet on myself.

At 22 I founded D-InfoTech Pvt Ltd in India. What set us apart wasn't just hardware and services. I built custom automation scripts that handled repetitive client tasks, saving them real time and money. That combination of hands-on infrastructure and software automation helped me grow from a solo operation to a team of 4 serving 30+ clients over 3 years.

I paused the business in 2024 to pursue my MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Northeastern University. The journey from fixing PCs as a kid to building a company that blended hardware and code taught me how to spot problems and ship real solutions. That's what I'm looking to do next.
